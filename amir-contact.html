FAQ

Common Questions

Answers to the questions Amir gets asked most often — from collaborators, journalists, students and policymakers.

Is Amir available for speaking engagements and conference presentations?
Yes — Amir has presented at ICAART (2024, 2025, 2026) and CEPE 2023 and welcomes invitations to academic conferences, industry panels, government forums, and public discussions on AI ethics, autonomous vehicles, and responsible technology design. Please use the contact form to discuss availability and logistics.
Can I access Amir's published papers for free?
Yes — the 2025 ICAART paper is available as open access via SCITEPRESS (DOI: 10.5220/0013123900003890). The 2023 CEPE paper is available via the IIT Chicago library. For the Emerald Publishing paper, please contact Amir directly via email and he will share a copy for personal academic use. Full citations and links are on the Publications page.
I'm a policymaker or regulator — how is this research relevant to my work?
Directly. The research produces four immediately implementable ethical principles (IOS, HCL, HPP, ODS) for national AV safety standards, and critiques Australia's 2018 NTC DRIS for excluding ethical criteria from safety assurance. Amir is keen to work with the National Transport Commission, state transport agencies, and automotive regulators to translate these findings into enforceable frameworks. Please reach out via email for a detailed policy briefing.
I'm a student or early-career researcher — can I reach out?
Absolutely. Amir enjoys supporting emerging researchers in AI ethics, philosophy of technology, and autonomous systems. Whether you're looking for guidance on your thesis, a reading list, or general career advice in interdisciplinary research, feel free to send a message. Response time may be slower for informal academic mentoring, but he does reply to genuine enquiries.
Is Amir open to co-authoring or collaborative grant applications?
Yes — research collaboration is actively welcomed. Amir is particularly interested in cross-institutional or industry-academic partnerships on Australian-focused AI ethics, AV regulation, and responsible AI design. If you have a project that aligns with these areas, please outline it in the contact form and he will respond to discuss feasibility and fit.
